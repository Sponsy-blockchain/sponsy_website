Description

The Nice Jazz Festival, held annually since 1948 in Nice, on the French Riviera, is “the first jazz festival of international significance.” At the inaugural festival, Louis Armstrong and his All Stars were the headliners. Frommer’s calls it “the biggest, flashiest, and most prestigious jazz festival in Europe.” Over the years, many artists, such as Lionel Hampton, Dizzy Gillespie, Ray Charles, Herbie Hancock, and Miles Davis, regularly appeared at the festival.
